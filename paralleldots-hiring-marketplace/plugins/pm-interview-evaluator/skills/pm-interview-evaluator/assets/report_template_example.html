What went well

  • Covered a JD-critical requirement. The JD leads with owning end-to-end delivery; the Bytemint deep-dive got real signal on exactly that.
  • Strong behavioural follow-up. On the milestone slip you didn't accept the first answer — pushing for what he personally decided surfaced genuine ownership rather than a rehearsed story.
  • Good use of an open scenario. Letting him think aloud on the two-clients-one-engineer prioritisation (transcript ~18 min) exposed clean structured thinking without leading him.
  • Right calibration on domain. A light touch on ShelfWatch was appropriate — you correctly didn't over-index on AI/ML depth, which is only 8% of the weighting.

What was missed / to improve

  • The highest-value axis — client handling — was never tested. No difficult-client scenario was run, so the 20%-weight stakeholder dimension is unproven.
    Should have asked: "A client says ShelfWatch is giving wrong numbers and wants to churn — walk me through your next 48 hours."
    To improve: always stage at least one hard-client scenario in a client-facing PM interview — it's too heavily weighted to leave out.
  • Motivation accepted at face value. "Want to grow in product delivery" is candidate-centric and wasn't drilled (transcript ~31 min). To improve: push generic motivation answers toward "why this company and role specifically."
  • A resume claim went unexamined. The cross-functional coordination claim got no concrete example. To improve: ask for one specific instance whenever a candidate asserts a skill without evidence.
