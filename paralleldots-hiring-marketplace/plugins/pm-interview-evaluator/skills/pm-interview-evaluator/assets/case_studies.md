# ParallelDots — Standard Round 2 Case Studies (repository)

This is the repository of the standard case studies used in the Round 2 (case study) round. **Each case study has its own scoring factors** — the factors that matter differ by case, so do not apply one fixed set to all of them.

At Round 2, ask the user which of these was given to the candidate (present the titles below as a **single-select dropdown**, plus an "Other / not listed" option), then score the candidate on **that case study's factors**. Compute the case study score as the straight average of that case study's factors, each out of 10.

---

## 1. CRM renewal strategy — InnovateX / GlobalRetail  *(Relationship / Account Management)*
An 18-month CRM client is up for renewal in 3 months; multi-stakeholder (VP Sales, Head of Marketing, CFO, CEO), CFO pushing for a discount, a competitor in play, slow adoption in marketing. Deliverables: a stakeholder-by-stakeholder renewal strategy + a strategic growth/adoption plan.

**Scoring factors**
- **Renewal strategy** — comprehensive, stakeholder-specific renewal plan that also leverages cross-sell / upsell.
- **Negotiation & objection handling** — handles the CFO's discount / TCO objection while protecting InnovateX's value.
- **Stakeholder & cross-functional management** — aligns Sales, Marketing, CFO and CEO, and drives adoption across departments.
- **Long-term / strategic vision** — forward-looking plan for the client's long-term success and growth.
- **Communication & presentation** — clarity and persuasiveness of the pitch.

## 2. Quarterly Business Review — Acme / TechSolutions  *(Relationship / Account Management)*
A 2-year client's renewal QBR with CTO, Head of Ops, Procurement, Product Owner and CEO; stable adoption but feature-adoption gaps and upsell openings. Deliverables: a QBR deck + a mock QBR role-play.

**Scoring factors**
- **QBR structure & execution** — structures and leads the QBR, communicates value, proposes forward-looking solutions.
- **Cross-sell / upsell** — identifies and positions new modules aligned to the client's strategic goals.
- **Stakeholder management** — engages and aligns stakeholders with competing priorities (Ops adoption vs Procurement cost).
- **Scope & delay handling** — manages out-of-scope feature requests and communicates rollout delays.
- **Communication & presentation** — quality of the QBR delivery and the role-play.

## 3. ShelfWatch delivery recovery — ParallelDots / ABC Corp  *(Project Management)*
An ongoing ShelfWatch multi-market rollout is 4 months late and 25% over budget from client scope creep; the sponsor is frustrated and evaluating another vendor. Deliverables: a data-driven "red" status narrative, a recovery plan, and a project governance strategy.

**Scoring factors**
- **Recovery plan quality** — creative, practical and feasible plan to get the project back on track.
- **Project governance** — a governance strategy (change control, cadence, decision rights) that supports the recovery.
- **Data-driven status narrative** — frames the "red" status to the sponsor with the right data, not excuses.
- **Difficult client conversations** — has the hard conversation directly and constructively; resets expectations.
- **Team & cross-team management** — motivates the team, handles a key resignation, manages tech-team coordination and escalations.
- **Communication & presentation** — explains complex project-management concepts clearly.

## 4. OTP login rollout — scrum planning  *(Product / Delivery)*
Move from password to OTP-based login on a product used by millions; scrum team, 2-week sprints, quarterly releases. Deliverables: a sequenced high-level activity plan, a stakeholder communication plan, a risk register with mitigations, stated assumptions, and a deck.

**Scoring factors**
- **Planning & sequencing** — correct, complete high-level activity breakdown before deployment.
- **Stakeholder identification & communication plan** — identifies all stakeholders and a plan to keep them informed.
- **Risk identification & mitigation** — surfaces real risks with credible mitigations.
- **Metrics & success measurement** — sprint metrics (speed / quality / responsiveness) and feature-success metrics.
- **Change handling & adaptability** — responds well to the mid-flight CISO requirement change.
- **Communication & presentation** — clarity of the deck and the reasoning (incl. stated assumptions).

## 5. CRM implementation for an EU retail chain  *(Project Management)*
Implement a CRM for a large European retail chain within 3 months; cross-cultural, time-zone, customization and local-compliance challenges. Deliverables: a project plan with milestones, a communications plan, a RAID log, stated assumptions, and a deck.

**Scoring factors**
- **Project planning** — clear, effective plan with milestones and deliverables.
- **Methodology & artifacts** — sound methodology choice with reasoning, and the right PM artifacts for the project.
- **Risk management (RAID)** — identifies and mitigates implementation risks.
- **Stakeholder management & communication** — cross-cultural / time-zone stakeholder communication.
- **Flexibility & change handling** — handles the mid-project scope/rework conversation and resets expectations.
- **Metrics & KPIs** — distinguishes internal vs client-facing success metrics.
- **Communication & presentation** — clarity of the deck and the reasoning.
